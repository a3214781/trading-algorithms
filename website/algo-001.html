Market Open
Inversion / IFVG
Identifies the session opening candle direction, then enters a counter-trend position when price shows either a 3-bar inversion fair value gap or a 50%+ reversal candle. Gated by an HTF bias filter.

HOW IT WORKS
1
Capture the Opening Candle
On session open (default 09:30 EST), the script records the first candle's high, low, direction (bull/bear), and absolute size in ticks. This candle defines the "bias" for that day — if it's bearish, we're looking for longs; if bullish, we're looking for shorts.
2
Check HTF Bias (30m)
Before any entry is allowed, the script checks the 30-minute chart's opening candle direction as a macro filter. This prevents trading against a strong HTF trend and reduces false signals. Configurable via counter-trend mode.
3
Watch for Trigger A — Inversion FVG (IFVG)
A 3-bar Fair Value Gap forms when bar[2].high < bar[0].low (bullish gap) or bar[2].low > bar[0].high (bearish gap). The script stores this zone. An inversion fires when price later closes back inside the gap from the opposite direction with a confirming close — e.g. price dips into a bullish FVG with a bearish close = bearish IFVG signal.
4
Watch for Trigger B — 50% Reversal Candle
Any subsequent candle that is ≥ 50% the size of the opening candle in the opposite direction triggers an entry. Example: 100 tick bearish open candle → a 50+ tick bullish candle = long entry. The threshold is configurable.
5
Entry, Stop Loss & Take Profit
Entry at close of trigger candle. Stop loss placed at the wick extreme of the entry candle (low for longs, high for shorts) plus a configurable tick buffer. Take profit is set at 0.5× the risk distance (negative RR — high WR, small winners). Break-even and trailing stop options available.

EDGE ANALYSIS

Why does this work? The opening candle on futures markets — especially MNQ — is frequently driven by overnight positioning, pre-market news, and institutional order flow. Price often makes an exaggerated initial move to sweep liquidity before reversing to the true intraday direction.

The IFVG entry specifically targets the moment when price returns to fill an imbalance created by that initial move, which is a well-documented institutional behaviour in ICT / Smart Money Concepts. The 50% candle trigger is a simpler momentum-based confirmation of the same reversal.

The 0.5 RR take profit is intentional — it prioritises win rate over profit per trade, which makes the strategy psychologically easier to follow and keeps the equity curve smooth. The trade-off is that one loss requires two wins to recover.

The break-even + trailing stop combination is doing significant work. These features protect winners and are a major contributor to the 94% WR — without them, raw entry quality may be lower.
